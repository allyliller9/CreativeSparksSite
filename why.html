Girls Deserve More Representation in STEM

  • Only 28% of the science and engineering workforce is female (National Science Foundation, 2023)
  • Girls are 5x less likely than boys to pursue computer science by high school (Code.org, 2023)
  • Women hold just 16% of engineering jobs (American Association of University Women, 2022)
  • STEM job growth is outpacing other fields by 8.8%, but the gender gap is widening (Bureau of Labor Statistics, 2023)
  • Only 24% of STEM workers are women, despite making up 50% of the college-educated workforce (Pew Research Center, 2023)
  • Women earn only 82 cents for every dollar earned by men in STEM fields (Census Bureau, 2022)
  • Girls who lose interest in STEM typically do so between ages 9-14 - our camp's target age range! (Microsoft Research, 2022)

The Power of Early Intervention

Summer camps and early STEM exposure make a huge difference!

  • Girls who participate in hands-on STEM activities before age 12 are 3x more likely to pursue STEM careers (National Girls Collaborative Project, 2023)
  • 83% of girls who attend STEM camps report increased confidence in math and science (Girl Scout Research Institute, 2022)
  • Early exposure to female STEM role models increases girls' likelihood of pursuing STEM by 42% (Harvard Business Review, 2023)
  • Girls who experience collaborative, creative STEM projects are 65% more likely to stay engaged through high school (AAUW Educational Foundation, 2022)
